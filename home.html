INTRODUCTION

Explores the idea that time is not a line but a state of awareness. There is no past to return to and no future to arrive at. There is only the present moment, constantly reshaping itself. Written with clarity and restraint, the story follows characters who begin to sense that what they call “now” is not fleeting, but central, a force that quietly governs memory, choice, and consequence. As perception shifts, ordinary experience gives way to something more expansive, where moments overlap and meaning is no longer fixed. The narrative is accessible and reflective, intended for readers of all ages, and especially for adolescents encountering these questions for the first time. Rather than offering answers, THE TIME BEING invites attention: to how time is felt, how it is inhabited, and how presence itself may be the only true measure of existence.
